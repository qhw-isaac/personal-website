09 Aug

MFRE Begins

The first week of my Master's summer bootcamp had just concluded. Perhaps it is a blessing that I only began sharing these notes now; I have been bored out of my mind the past 2 months as I finished Calculus I in June. Should I have shared these notes any earlier, I would be no less than polluting this brand new corner of the internet. As I digress, my rediscovery of Mathematics has brought me some of the greatest joys this past year. Thankfully, this summer bootcamp has been very intellectually enriching in its absence.

Interestingly, I've found myself struggling to connect socially more than I did before. I'm uncertain if it's due to any external circumstances or if I'm molding into the stereotype of the "nerd" I've become. One thing I know for certain is I need to stop correcting professors in class before they conjure an aversive response when they see me raise my hand; it's so hard to help it though, seeing broken patterns irks me! Alas, let the metamorphosis begin; I hope I come out the other side a person I continue to be proud of.

I've been humbled. Though there is a great lesson or perhaps a much needed reminder here before I fully embrace Economics. Intuitively, I can feel the lesson ruminating... sometimes the conclusion is so believable yet cannot be proven that we just have to consider the possibility that it cannot be false. 16 Aug

Networking Barbecue

I met Cam, an entrepreneur in residence at Innovation UBC, at a barbecue hosted by my partner's friend. The barbecue itself was interesting, an amalgamation of friends but also for the purposes of social networking... not particularly an environment I enjoy. However, my conversation with Cam did inspire me to consider the fact that perhaps I'm lost amongst the four paths laid out before me (academia, government, NGO, and industry) because none of which fully satisfies me, at least as an working enconomist. Cam suggested the possibility that I could try and merge my experience in leadership and entrepreneurship with my passion for animal welfare and knowledge in economics into some type of scalable product. This is super fascinating as I ruminate on my MFRE graduating project; perhaps I could pivot towards gaining experience that allows me to design a product that can both benefit industry and better animal welfare. Lots to think about!

29 Aug

Ruckle Heritage Farm

I had the privilege of visiting Ruckle Heritage Farm on Salt Spring Island the last weekend before school began. It wasn't necessarily planned to center the trip around agriculture, but it ended up being quite an eye-opening experience. The folks we stayed with at the farm (Marjorie, Mike, Matt, and Abey) gave us a tour and shared their family's history managing the land. It was incredible witnessing a farm centered around the philosophy of sustainable agriculture and land stewardship both succeed and thrive. It was even moreso incredible to see animals in agriculture living as part of a greater ecosystem that the folks running the farm consider themselves to be a part of.
